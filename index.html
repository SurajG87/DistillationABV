How to Make Sugar Syrup

  1. Measure sugar and water according to the ratio selected (typically 2:1 sugar:water)
  2. Heat water in a clean saucepan until warm but not boiling (60-70¬∞C / 140-160¬∞F)
  3. Add sugar slowly while stirring continuously until fully dissolved
  4. Do NOT boil as this can create crystallization issues later
  5. Cool completely to room temperature before adding to spirit
  6. Add syrup gradually to your spirit, tasting as you go
  7. Let it rest for 1-2 weeks to allow flavors to integrate
  8. Filter if needed through coffee filter for clarity
Common Syrup Ratios
1:1 (Simple Syrup): Equal parts sugar and water. Thin consistency, 50% sugar.
2:1 (Rich Syrup): Twice as much sugar as water. Thicker, 67% sugar. RECOMMENDED for spirits.
3:1 (Very Rich): Three parts sugar to one water. Very thick, 75% sugar.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Measure your distillate precisely using a graduated cylinder

Important Notes

Never add dry sugar: Sugar won't dissolve in high-proof alcohol. Always make syrup first.
Syrup temperature: Always cool syrup to room temp before adding to alcohol to prevent evaporation.
Aging period: Sweetened spirits need 1-2 weeks to integrate flavors properly.
Test small batches: Always make a small test batch before scaling up your recipe.
